Frequently Asked Questions

What score do I need to get a 5 on AP Calc AB?

Historically, you need approximately 68-70% of the composite score, which translates to around 74 out of 108 points. This means you could get roughly 32-33 correct on the MC section and 32-33 points on the FRQ section combined.

Is there a penalty for wrong answers on the MC section?

No! There is no penalty for guessing on the AP Calculus AB exam. You should answer every question, even if you need to guess. Only correct answers count toward your raw scoreâ€”blank or incorrect answers are scored the same (0 points).

What's the difference between AP Calc AB and BC?

AP Calculus AB covers roughly the equivalent of a first-semester college calculus course (limits, derivatives, integrals, and FTC). AP Calculus BC covers all AB topics PLUS additional content (parametric/polar/vector functions, series, Taylor polynomials) equivalent to a full year of college calculus.

What calculator is allowed on the AP Calc AB exam?

You may use a graphing calculator on Part B of the MC section and all of Section II (FRQ). Popular choices include the TI-84, TI-89, TI-Nspire, and Casio ClassPad. Your calculator must be able to graph functions, find zeros, and calculate numerical derivatives/integrals. Check the College Board calculator policy for the full list.

Most colleges grant credit for scores of 3, 4, or 5, though policies vary widely. Some selective schools only accept 4s and 5s, while others accept 3s.
